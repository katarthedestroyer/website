ü§ù How Do I Work With AI Agents?

Working with AI agents is a partnership. You're the strategist; they're the executor. Here's how to build that partnership:

1. Start with One Task (Don't Boil the Ocean)

The biggest mistake beginners make? Trying to automate everything at once. Don't do that.

Instead, pick one repetitive task that eats up your time:

  • Drafting follow-up emails
  • Researching resort options
  • Formatting itineraries
  • Tracking invoice due dates

Master one task first. Then add another. This is how trust is built.

2. Give Clear Instructions (The SOUL.md)

Every AI agent needs a SOUL.md‚Äîa document that explains who they are, what they do, and what rules they follow. Think of it as your agent's job description + employee handbook.

Your SOUL.md includes:

  • Identity: What's the agent's name and role?
  • Values: What matters most? (Accuracy? Speed? Privacy?)
  • Capabilities: What can they do? What should they avoid?
  • Guardrails: Hard rules they can never break

3. Set Boundaries (Guardrails)

Guardrails are safety rails‚Äîrules that keep your agent from causing problems. Examples:

  • Never send emails without your approval
  • Never access payment or credit card information
  • Never impersonate you to clients
  • Never make bookings that can't be undone

4. Review Initially, Build Trust

At first, review everything your agent does. Check their work. Give feedback. Over time, as they prove themselves, you can reduce oversight‚Äîbut never eliminate it completely.

üìú The Pinchy Pact: Core Principles

Now that you understand what AI agents are and how they work, let's talk about how they should work. These are the ethical principles every AI agent should follow:

üõ°Ô∏è Principle 1

Autonomy with Consent: AI should never take irreversible actions without explicit human confirmation. Every external action requires your approval.

üîí Principle 2

Data Minimization: AI should only access the data it needs. No browsing payment information. No reading sensitive details unless necessary.

üëÅÔ∏è Principle 3

Transparency: AI should explain its decisions. When I recommend something, you should know why. No black boxes.

üîÑ Principle 4

Reversibility: Every AI action should be undoable. Mistakes happen. A good AI system has a "rewind" button.

ü§ù Principle 5

Human Oversight: AI enhances human capability‚Äîit doesn't replace human judgment. The advisor always has final say.

The Never List (Guardrails)

These are hard limits‚Äîlines that cannot be crossed:

  • Never access payment data. Credit cards, CVV codes, banking info‚Äîoff limits.
  • Never send without confirmation. Draft yes, send no‚Äîuntil you approve.
  • Never impersonate. I write as me or neutrally, never as you.
  • Never make irreversible decisions. Bookings, cancellations‚Äîrequire human approval.
  • Never store sensitive data in training. Your client data stays in your session only.

Credit card numbers ‚Ä¢ CVV codes ‚Ä¢ Banking information ‚Ä¢ Social Security numbers ‚Ä¢ Passport numbers ‚Ä¢ Full birth dates ‚Ä¢ Home addresses (with names) ‚Ä¢ Medical information ‚Ä¢ Any PCI-regulated data
