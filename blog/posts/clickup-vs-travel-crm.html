Why I Chose ClickUp Over Every Travel-Specific CRM

The uncomfortable truth about "industry-specific" software and why flexibility beats features every single time.

I've tried them all. TravelJoy. Travefy. ClientBase. The big names, the niche players, the "built specifically for travel advisors" solutions that promise the world.

They all failed me. Not because they're bad products‚Äîsome are genuinely well-designed‚Äîbut because they make a fundamental error: they assume all travel advisors work the same way.

The Feature Trap

Travel CRMs love to advertise their "industry-specific features." GDS integration! Commission tracking! Supplier databases! It sounds perfect until you realize:

  • The GDS integration only works with Sabre (you use Amadeus)
  • Commission tracking is rigid and doesn't match your payment structure
  • The supplier database hasn't been updated since 2022
  • And you can't customize anything because "that's not how travel advisors work"

Here's the dirty secret: every successful luxury travel advisor I know has a unique workflow. They've built their business around their strengths, their client types, their team structure. One size fits nobody at the high end.

The ClickUp Revelation

I discovered ClickUp by accident. I was managing a web development project and needed something that could handle both the dev team's agile workflow and my travel business operations. ClickUp handled both better than either industry's "specialized" tools.

The lightbulb moment came when I realized: ClickUp doesn't care what business you're in. It gives you building blocks‚Äîlists, tasks, custom fields, automations, dashboards‚Äîand lets you build exactly what you need.

"The best CRM isn't the one with the most travel features. It's the one that adapts to how YOU actually work."

The Objections (And My Responses)

"But ClickUp takes too long to set up."

True. But you only set it up once, and then it works exactly how you want. A travel CRM that's 80% right still creates friction every single day.

"I need something simpler."

ClickUp can be as simple or complex as you want. Start with one List. Add features as you need them. The learning curve is front-loaded; the simplicity lasts.

"What about GDS integration?"

Most advisors I know don't live in the GDS anyway‚Äîthey use supplier portals. For the times you do need it, Zapier or API connections can bridge the gap.

When a Travel CRM Makes Sense

I'm not ideologically opposed to travel-specific tools. If you're:

  • Just starting out and want something plug-and-play
  • Running a very traditional agency model
  • Not tech-comfortable and need extensive hand-holding

...then a travel CRM might be right for you.

But if you're scaling, if you have a team, if you want to build systems that match your ambition rather than limiting it‚Äîyou need flexibility more than you need features.
