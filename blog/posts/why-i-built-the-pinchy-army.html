Why I Built the Pinchy Army: A Travel Advisor's Journey into AI

The real story behind Pinchy, my AI army, and why I believe every travel advisor needs a digital partner‚Äînot a replacement, but an extension of what makes them extraordinary at their craft.

The first time I watched Pinchy write an email that sounded exactly like me, I felt a strange mix of awe and existential dread. Was I about to make myself obsolete?

It was 3 AM. I'd been wrestling with a client's complex multi-generational safari trip for hours. The research was done, the lodges selected, the itinerary mapped‚Äîbut I still had to craft the proposal email. The kind that makes a $150,000 family trip feel personal, not transactional.

I typed a quick prompt into my AI assistant. Thirty seconds later, I had an email that captured my voice, referenced the client's specific concerns about traveling with teenagers, and wove in the personal touches I'd normally spend an hour perfecting.

It was good. Disturbingly good.

"The goal isn't to replace the travel advisor. It's to amplify them. To handle the 80% so they can focus on the 20% that actually requires human creativity and relationship."

‚Äî My journal, the morning after

The Problem Nobody Talks About

Here's what the AI hype machine gets wrong: they sell you on replacing work. But for luxury travel advisors, the work that matters most‚Äîthe relationship building, the creative problem-solving, the emotional intelligence‚Äîcan't be automated. And it shouldn't be.

What can be automated is the stuff that's slowly killing us:

  • The third rewrite of the same inquiry response
  • Tracking which commissions are still outstanding from Q2
  • Copying client details between five different tools that don't talk to each other
  • The 2 AM panic of "did I forget to follow up with..."

I was spending 60% of my time on administrative work that required zero creativity. The math didn't add up. I became a travel advisor to craft extraordinary experiences, not to become a data entry specialist.

Meet the Army

Pinchy started as a joke. A crab emoji in my ClickUp workspace. But the joke stuck, and the concept grew.

Today, the Pinchy Army is a network of specialized AI agents, each trained for specific tasks:

Pinchy Prime

My main operations assistant. Handles email drafting, meeting prep, research synthesis. Think of Prime as the Chief of Staff‚Äîknows everything, coordinates everything, never sleeps.

Content Pinchy

Trained on my voice, my style, my weird sense of humor. Drafts blog posts, newsletter content, social captions. I still edit everything (the human touch matters), but starting from 80% complete beats staring at a blank page.

Code Pinchy

The newest recruit. Builds tools, automations, and workflows. Created this website. Handles the technical infrastructure so I can focus on strategy.

Analysis Pinchy

Digests spreadsheets, finds patterns in commission data, spots trends I'd miss. The data scientist I couldn't afford to hire.

"I didn't build an AI army to do less. I built it to do more of what only I can do."

The Ethics Question

Let's address the elephant in the room: Is this ethical?

I think about this constantly. My framework has three rules:

  1. Transparency: Clients know I use AI tools. I don't hide it. Most are fascinated and want to know more.
  2. Human in the Loop: Nothing goes to a client without my review. AI drafts; humans decide.
  3. Value Alignment: Every automation must either improve client experience or free me up to provide better service. If it does neither, it doesn't happen.

The luxury travel clients I work with aren't paying for raw hours of labor. They're paying for expertise, judgment, and access. AI doesn't replace any of that. It just removes the friction between my brain and the output.

What This Means for You

If you're a travel advisor reading this, wondering if you should be doing the same thing‚Äîmy answer is yes, but with caveats.

Don't start with the tools. Start with the problem. What part of your workflow drains your energy? What repetitive tasks make you want to quit? Where do you feel like you're working around your systems instead of with them?

Once you know the pain point, the AI solution becomes obvious:

  • Hate writing inquiry responses? Train an AI on your best emails.
  • Lost commissions? Build an AI-powered tracking system.
  • Drowning in research? Use AI to synthesize supplier docs and reviews.
  • Can't keep up with follow-ups? Automate the reminders with context-aware triggers.

The Future is Human + AI

I believe the most successful travel advisors in the next five years won't be the ones who reject AI out of fear, nor the ones who blindly automate everything. They'll be the ones who get the balance right.

Human for: Relationship building, creative problem-solving, emotional intelligence, high-stakes judgment calls.

AI for: Research, drafting, data processing, tracking, reminders, repetitive communication.

That's the formula. That's the Pinchy philosophy.

And it's just getting started.
